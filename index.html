Fufu and Eru Recipe

Eru and Water Fufu is a signature Cameroonian dish, originating from the Bayangi people in the South West region. It is a nutritious, high-protein mix of finely shredded eru leaves and Waterleaf, cooked with palm oil, smoked fish, crayfish, and cow skin.

Ingredients and Cooking Approach

  • Eru (Vegetable): Finely shredded leaves cooked until soft.
  • Waterleaf/Spinach: Adds moisture and balance.
  • Proteins: Smoked fish, beef, tripe, and cow skin.
  • Base: Palm oil and crayfish.
  • Water Fufu: Made from cassava flour and water.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Wash and boil meat until it becomes tender.
  2. Add your washed eru and waterleaf and cook until soft.
  3. Add crayfish, pepper, and seasoning, and stir using a wooden spoon.
  4. Add meat, fish, and cow skin and stir to mix the ingredients.
  5. Add palm oil and let to simmer.

Now we prepare the fufu:

  1. Pour fufu powder into boiling water.
  2. Stir until smooth and thick. Normally, at this point, the color of the fuf should look slightly brown.

Do not overcook eru leaves to maintain nutrients.

Nutrition Facts (Approximate)

Rich in protein, fiber, and healthy fats. Provides essential vitamins and minerals.
